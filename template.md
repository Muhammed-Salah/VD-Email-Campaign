# Email Campaign Templates Analysis

This document outlines the current email templates used in the **Kerala Speaks** campaign and proposes additional templates for review.

## 1. Core Metadata
- **Recipients:** `office@rahulgandhi.in`, `connect@inc.in`
- **Primary Subject:** `Request: Appoint V.D. Satheeshan as Chief Minister of Kerala`

---

## 2. Reason Paragraphs (Dynamic Content)
These paragraphs are injected into the templates based on the user's selection in the "Why do you support Satheeshan?" section.

| Key | Title | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **accountability** | Accountability | Over the past five years, Satheeshan led the opposition in the Kerala Assembly with rare consistency — questioning every policy failure, pressing every minister on record, and doing so without theatrics. Kerala needs that same discipline in its Chief Minister. |
| **organisation** | Grassroots Strength | What sets Satheeshan apart is what he did when Congress was at its lowest. He did not manage the decline — he reversed it. He rebuilt the party's presence district by district, booth by booth, and turned a demoralised organisation into one that just secured nearly 100 seats. |
| **integrity** | Integrity | Satheeshan publicly staked his career on this election — promising to "quit politics and go into exile" if the UDF did not win 100 seats. The UDF won 98. That kind of personal accountability before the people is rare in Indian politics, and it is exactly what the Chief Minister's office demands. |
| **promise** | People's Choice | The UDF's decisive victory is inseparable from Satheeshan's leadership of the campaign. The mandate the people gave is a mandate for him. The Chief Minister's office should follow naturally from that. |
| **default** | - | Satheeshan led the UDF's campaign with clarity and personal accountability. He rebuilt the Congress organisation across Kerala over five years in opposition, and the result — 98 seats for the UDF — is a direct reflection of that leadership. |

## 3. Existing Email Templates

### Template 1: Formal
**Tone:** Respectful, structured, and institutional.
```text
Dear Rahul Gandhi Ji and INC Leadership,

I am writing to you as [Name/District Context], and I wish to place on record my strong support for V.D. Satheeshan to be appointed as the next Chief Minister of Kerala.

[Injected Reason Paragraph]

The UDF's victory after a decade in opposition is significant. Honouring it by choosing the leader who engineered that victory would send the right message — to Kerala and to the country.

I respectfully urge you to appoint V.D. Satheeshan as Kerala's Chief Minister. Kerala is watching, and I believe this is the right decision.

Thank you for your attention.

Warm regards,
[Name]
[District, Kerala]
```

## 4. Proposed New Templates for Review

```text

I am writing as [Name/District Context] to express the aspirations of a new generation of Keralites. We have watched V.D. Satheeshan modernise the party's approach and speak the language of progress and development.

[Injected Reason Paragraph]

I urge you to confirm his leadership as Chief Minister.

Respectfully,
[Name]
[District, Kerala]
```
